Tinglar

The tinglar, Puerto Rico's endangered leatherback sea turtle, is a gentle giant of the ocean

November 1, 2015 Gabriel Pena

The tinglar, or leatherback sea turtle, is the largest sea turtle species in the world and a revered creature in Puerto Rico. Known for its unique, leathery shell and impressive size, the tinglar can grow up to seven feet long and weigh over 2,000 pounds. These gentle giants frequent the island’s beaches, particularly between March and August, as they nest along Puerto Rico’s eastern and northern shores. Observing these magnificent creatures nesting under the moonlight is a memorable experience and a reminder of the island's diverse marine life. Despite their awe-inspiring presence, tinglars are critically endangered due to threats like habitat loss, plastic pollution, and climate change. Puerto Rico has become a key player in conservation efforts, with organizations and volunteers working tirelessly to protect nesting sites and support hatchlings on their journey to the sea. This collective effort ensures that future generations can continue to witness the tinglar’s legacy on the island’s shores, highlighting the importance of preserving Puerto Rico’s natural wonders.
